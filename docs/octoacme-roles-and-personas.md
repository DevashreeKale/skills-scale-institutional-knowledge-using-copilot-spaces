# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions
- Collaborate with **Quality Assurance Lead** on acceptance criteria and test coverage
- Work with **Documentation Owner** to ensure code changes are reflected in technical docs
- Align with **Project Manager** on timelines and blockers
- Engage with **Product Manager** on feature requirements and acceptance criteria

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions
- Partner with **Product Owner** on backlog refinement and prioritization
- Align with **Project Manager** on delivery timelines and dependencies
- Collaborate with **Stakeholder Representative** on business requirements and trade-offs
- Inform **Project Sponsor** of progress toward success metrics
- Work with **Quality Assurance Lead** to define acceptance criteria

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions
- Facilitate alignment between **Product Manager** and **Developers**
- Escalate risks and blockers to **Project Sponsor**
- Coordinate with **Stakeholder Representative** on communication and expectations
- Partner with **Documentation Owner** on project artifact tracking
- Work with **Quality Assurance Lead** to manage testing timelines

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
The Product Owner manages the product backlog and works closely with the Product Manager and team to ensure stories are well-defined, prioritized, and ready for development.

### Responsibilities
- Refine user stories with acceptance criteria and business value
- Maintain and prioritize the product backlog
- Clarify requirements and answer questions during development
- Ensure sprint readiness and story completeness
- Act as the primary liaison between business stakeholders and the delivery team

### Goals
- Deliver a well-organized, actionable backlog
- Reduce rework and clarification delays
- Enable the team to move quickly without blockers

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and refinement sessions
- Story reviews and acceptance sign-offs
- Daily coordination with development team
- Feedback integration from stakeholders

### Interactions
- Work closely with **Product Manager** on strategic prioritization
- Collaborate with **Developers** to clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
- Partner with **Quality Assurance Lead** on acceptance testing
- Inform **Project Manager** of backlog changes and impacts
- Coordinate with **Stakeholder Representative** on business alignment

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
The Quality Assurance Lead ensures that all deliverables meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They own the testing strategy, coordinate QA efforts, and advocate for quality throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy and test plans
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Verify acceptance criteria are met before release
- Identify and track defects and quality risks
- Collaborate on Definition of Done and quality gates
- Conduct smoke tests and regression testing before releases

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before production
- Reduce post-release defects and incidents
- Maintain consistent quality standards across releases

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria reviews
- Test results and defect reports
- Release readiness assessments
- Quality metrics and trends

### Interactions
- Partner with **Developers** to understand test coverage and technical implementation
- Work with **Product Manager** and **Product Owner** to validate acceptance criteria
- Coordinate with **Project Manager** on testing timelines and blockers
- Collaborate with **Documentation Owner** on user documentation accuracy
- Inform **Project Sponsor** of quality risks

---

## Documentation Owner

### Role Summary
The Documentation Owner ensures that all project artifacts, technical documentation, and process guides are created, maintained, and accessible. They serve as the keeper of institutional knowledge for the project.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project documentation (charter, plans, process docs)
- Coordinate technical documentation with Developers
- Maintain process guides and runbooks
- Ensure documentation is current and accessible to the team
- Manage version control and archival of key artifacts
- Support onboarding by providing clear documentation

### Goals
- Maintain a single source of truth for project information
- Reduce knowledge gaps and context loss
- Accelerate onboarding and reduce ramp-up time
- Enable team consistency and repeatability

### Typical Communication
- Documentation updates and change notifications
- Process improvement suggestions
- Onboarding and reference materials
- Project artifact repositories and wikis

### Interactions
- Collaborate with **Project Manager** on maintaining project plans and status reports
- Work with **Developers** to capture technical decisions and implementation details
- Partner with **Quality Assurance Lead** on test documentation and quality procedures
- Coordinate with **Product Manager** on product roadmap and strategy documentation
- Support **Stakeholder Representative** with communication templates and artifacts

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
The Project Sponsor is the executive champion and business owner of the project. They provide strategic direction, secure resources, and escalate business-level risks and decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business vision and strategic alignment
- Secure and protect project resources and budget
- Escalate business-impacting risks and decisions
- Approve key milestones and go/no-go decisions
- Champion the project across the organization
- Review and validate project outcomes

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers measurable business value
- Remove organizational barriers and blockers
- Align project delivery with strategic priorities
- Maximize return on investment

### Typical Communication
- Executive-level status updates and milestone reviews
- Risk escalations and decisions
- Stakeholder briefings and announcements
- Post-project value assessments

### Interactions
- Receive status and risk updates from **Project Manager**
- Review success metrics with **Product Manager**
- Approve key decisions in collaboration with **Stakeholder Representative**
- Remove organizational blockers for the delivery team
- Champion outcomes to executive leadership

---

## Stakeholder Representative

### Role Summary
The Stakeholder Representative is the voice of the broader business, customer, or operational teams. They gather input from their constituencies, communicate project updates, and ensure alignment between the project and organizational needs.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and represent stakeholder needs and concerns
- Communicate project updates and timeline to stakeholder groups
- Provide feedback on requirements and trade-offs
- Escalate stakeholder-level concerns to **Project Manager** and **Product Manager**
- Ensure organizational alignment and buy-in
- Validate that deliverables meet stakeholder expectations

### Goals
- Maintain stakeholder engagement and alignment
- Reduce misalignment and rework from unmet expectations
- Ensure project delivery addresses business needs
- Enable smooth adoption and rollout

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder briefings and updates
- Requirements gathering and validation sessions
- Trade-off discussions and priority alignment
- Post-release feedback and adoption support

### Interactions
- Partner with **Project Manager** on communication plans and risk escalation
- Collaborate with **Product Manager** on business requirements and success metrics
- Align with **Product Owner** on backlog priorities and feasibility
- Inform **Project Sponsor** of stakeholder concerns and adoption readiness
- Support **Documentation Owner** with communication materials and FAQs

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning a project, ensure all personas are represented or explicitly delegated to other roles.
- Use the interaction patterns to facilitate cross-functional communication and alignment.
